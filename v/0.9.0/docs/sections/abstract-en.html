Abstract

The core ontology of the circular factory defines the core concepts required to represent the state of a circular factory, integrating linear production with circular processes such as disassembly, reprocessing, and reassembly. It provides a structured and extensible representation of relevant entities, attributes, and relationships needed to capture dynamic and uncertain material flows, including the coexistence of new and reused components and resulting state transitions. Designed as a central integration layer, the ontology connects specialized ontologies for resources such as production stations and intralogistics systems while maintaining a consistent level of abstraction. This enables a unified and interoperable description of circular factory systems for analysis, monitoring, and control.
