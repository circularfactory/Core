Circular Factory Core Ontology: Description back to ToC

In circular factories, products are manufactured by combining newly produced components with reprocessed components recovered from previous product generations, which introduces a high degree of uncertainty with respect to component availability, quality, and required processing steps. This is particularly characteristic of remanufacturing systems, where the outcomes of inspection and disassembly processes directly influence subsequent routing, resource allocation, and process planning decisions. As a result, circular production systems exhibit dynamic and interdependent material flows that require continuous adaptation at both the operational and control levels. To enable consistent decision-making across these levels, a shared semantic representation of products, resources, processes, and their states is required. Building on the remanufacturing ontology proposed in [1], which establishes core concepts for adaptive remanufacturing systems, the present ontology extends this foundation to the broader context of circular factories and serves as a central integration layer for more specialized ontologies, such as those describing production resources and intralogistics systems. In this context, it aligns with the requirements of circular factory control architectures, where robust and flexible coordination of production, inspection, and intralogistics is necessary to manage uncertainty [2], enable real-time responsiveness, and ensure robust and resilient system operation.
